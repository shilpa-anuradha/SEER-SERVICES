NOTES OF AIRCRAFT

  • C-130 Hercules

    C-130 Hercules is a four-engine turboprop military transport/cargo aircraft designed and built originally by USA. Presently more than 2500 units are in operation all over the world. This Aircraft is capable of using unprepared runways for short take-off and landings.

  • Beech King Air 200

    The Beech Super King Air is available with government, military and commercial customers for special mission operations such as aerial survey, air ambulance, flight inspection and surveillance, installed on twin turbo propeller engines of Pratt & Whitney.
    About 3800 units are being operated around the world.

  • K-8

    The Hongdu JL-8 (Nanchang JL-8), also known as the Karakorum-8 or K-8 for short, is a two-seat intermediate jet trainer and light attack aircraft designed in the People's Republic of China.

  • Y-12

    The Y-12 is a high wing twin-engine turboprop utility aircraft built by in China and powered by two turbo prop Pratt & Whitney PT-6A-27 engines Canada. Production starts in 1985.operated by government, military and civil customers.

  • MI-17

    The Mil Mi-17 is a Russian helicopter is manufactured by twin factories in Russia. It is a medium twin-turbine transport helicopter. Developed from the basic Mi-8 airframe, the Mi-17 was fitted with the larger Klimov TV3-117MT engines, rotors, and transmission along with fuselage improvements for heavier loads. Optional engines for "hot and high" conditions.

  • MI-24

    Mi-24 helicopter is a large helicopter gunship, attack helicopter and low-capacity troop transport with room for eight passengers. It is produced by Russia and has been operating since 1972 with more than 30 other nations.
